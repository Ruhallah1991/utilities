CMOS Formulas Overview

CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) transistors are foundational in integrated circuit design.

Transconductance (gm)

For NMOS and PMOS:

\[ gm = \frac{2I_D}{V_{GS} - V_{TH}} \]

Where \(I_D\) is the drain current, \(V_{GS}\) is the gate-to-source voltage, and \(V_{TH}\) is the threshold voltage.

Output Resistance (ro)

\[ ro = \frac{1}{\lambda I_D} \]

Where \(\lambda\) is the channel-length modulation parameter and \(I_D\) is the drain current.

Drain-to-Source Conductance (gds)

\[ gds = \lambda I_D \]

Where \(\lambda\) and \(I_D\) are the same as above.

Body Effect Transconductance (gmb)

For NMOS:

\[ gmb = -1 \times gm \times \frac{\gamma}{2 \times \sqrt{2\phi + V_{SB}}} \]

Where \(\gamma\) is the body effect coefficient, \(\phi\) is the bulk Fermi potential, and \(V_{SB}\) is the source-to-body voltage.

Additional Definitions of Transconductance

In Saturation

\[ gm = \sqrt{2 \mu C_{ox} \frac{W}{L} I_D} \]

Where \(\mu\) is the carrier mobility, \(C_{ox}\) is the oxide capacitance, \(W\) and \(L\) are the width and length of the transistor channel, and \(I_D\) is the drain current.

In Triode

\[ gm = \mu C_{ox} \frac{W}{L} (V_{GS} - V_{TH} - V_{DS}) \]

Where \(V_{DS}\) is the drain-to-source voltage and other parameters are as defined above.

Weak Inversion

\[ gm = \frac{I_D}{nV_T} \]

Where \(n\) is the subthreshold slope factor and \(V_T\) is the thermal voltage.

Strong Inversion

\[ gm = \frac{2I_D}{V_{GS} - V_{TH}} \]

Same as the general expression but specifically for the strong inversion region.

JFETs and Other Devices

\[ gm = \frac{2I_D}{V_{P}} \]

Where \(V_{P}\) is the pinch-off voltage.
