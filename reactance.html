Formulas

Inductive reactance: \(X_L = 2\pi f L\) where \(f\) is frequency in Hz and \(L\) is inductance in henries (H).

Capacitive reactance: \(X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi f C}\) where \(f\) is frequency in Hz and \(C\) is capacitance in farads (F).

To calculate inductance: \(L = \frac{X_L}{2\pi f}\) where \(X_L\) is inductive reactance in ohms (\(\Omega\)) and \(f\) is frequency in Hz.

To calculate capacitance: \(C = \frac{1}{2\pi f X_C}\) where \(X_C\) is capacitive reactance in ohms (\(\Omega\)) and \(f\) is frequency in Hz.

Definitions

Inductive reactance is the opposition to current flow through an inductor. It is proportional to inductance and frequency.

Capacitive reactance is the opposition to current flow through a capacitor. It is inversely proportional to capacitance and frequency.

Inductance (L) is a property of an inductor and a measure of its ability to store energy in a magnetic field. Unit is henry (H).

Capacitance (C) is a property of a capacitor and a measure of its ability to store energy in an electric field. Unit is farad (F).
